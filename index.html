Welcome to YINET Uganda

"To provide first class youth services that supports the personal and social development of young people, enabling them to increase their resilience, learn new skills in the present, and promote their ambition for the future."
"To support all young people, especially those who needs us most to reach their full potential and make positive life choices by engaging in fun and innovative youth activities to move from dependence to interdependence."

Our Profile

Youth Inclusion Network Uganda (YINET Uganda) is a refugee-led Community Based Organization (CBO) started in 2020 and registered on 12th May 2023 (Reg. No. ODU/07/2023) in Terego District.

Our Background

YINET Uganda is a youth-founded, refugee-led community-based organization working in partnership with women-led CBOs/CSOs, local governments, national and international NGOs, and church-based organizations. Established in Imvepi Refugee Settlement, Odupi Sub-County, Terego District, we are committed to strengthening women and girlsâ€™ capacities for improved menstrual health, SRHR, education, and youth civic engagement.

Our Goal

To be a leading youth-led development agency championing women and girls' rights access to menstrual health, sexual reproductive health rights, and meaningful youth engagement in the civic and democratic space for sustainable development.

Our Programs

Dedicated thematic areas addressing key challenges faced by youth, women, and girls.

Menstrual Health Management (MHM)

  • Awareness and education on menstrual hygiene.
  • Training on production of local reusable sanitary pads.
  • Engagement with religious and cultural leaders.
  • Strengthening community and school structures.

Sexual Reproductive Health Rights (SRHR)

  • HIV awareness creation.
  • Reproductive health education and family planning.
  • Sex education for in- and out-of-school youth.

Youth & Civic Engagement

  • Mentorship on leadership, governance, and innovation.
  • Youth empowerment for sustainable livelihoods.
  • Training on computer skills and knowledge.

Education

  • Play-based early childhood development.
  • Child-friendly learning spaces.
  • Sessions for children and parents.
